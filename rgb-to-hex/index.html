Hex colors use a # prefix followed by 6 hexadecimal digits: the first two are Red, the middle two are Green, and the last two are Blue. For example, #ff6600 is orange.
Each RGB channel (Red, Green, Blue) ranges from 0 to 255. 0 means no contribution from that channel, 255 is full intensity. Together they can represent over 16 million colors.
